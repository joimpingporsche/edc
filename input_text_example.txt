TITLE: Definition: Digital Solution
CONTENT:
In alignment with the Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe) and extended by Porsche-specific terminology, a Digital Solution is defined as follows:
A Digital Solution is a cohesive construct that delivers business value by integrating multiple IT Products. These IT Products are modular, technically and logically bounded units that implement specific business functionalities. Together, they form the technical foundation of a Digital Solution.
In addition to IT Products, a Digital Solution may also include:
• Business processes • Methods and practices • Organizational structures
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Characteristics of a Digital Solution
CONTENT:
A Digital Solution:
• Consists of IT Products Each IT Product is an application, business service, or business platform, including its associated technologies. These are delivered to internal users (employees) and/or external users (customers).
• Supports Business Capabilities IT Products enable business capabilities, processes, and data entities.
• Enables Operational Value Streams (OVS) Digital Solutions are designed to support and enhance OVS by delivering measurable business value.
• Is Developed Within a Single Agile Release Train (ART) Each Digital Solution is budgeted and managed within one portfolio and is developed by one or more agile teams within a single ART.
• Is Part of a Development Value Stream (DVS) The contributing teams are part of a DVS and are responsible for defining, building, validating, and releasing the IT Products that make up the Digital Solution.
• Supports Independent Delivery IT Products within a Digital Solution are potentially independently deployable and operable, ensuring resilience, scalability, and speed.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Purpose and Benefits
CONTENT:
• A Digital Solution delivers end-to-end value to internal or external customers.
• Customers do not purchase isolated features or capabilities—they seek complete solutions that fulfill their desired outcomes.
• Digital Solutions enable organizations to align technical delivery with business strategy and customer needs.

[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Introduction
CONTENT:
At Porsche AG and its subsidiaries, all business processes are modeled and maintained exclusively by designated process owners using the BIC Process Design tool.
In LeanIX, no new business processes are created or modified. All process- related modeling activities—including the assignment of IT products to process activities—are performed solely in BIC.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: General Modeling Rules
CONTENT:
• The "Process" fact sheet in LeanIX is read-only and does not follow a dedicated modeling guideline.
• Business processes are not modeled or edited directly in LeanIX.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: System Integration: LeanIX ↔ BIC
CONTENT:
To support enterprise architecture modeling, especially the business architecture layer, LeanIX relies on the availability of active business processes. For this reason, a synchronization interface is in place:
Process Import from BIC to LeanIX • All active business processes modeled in BIC are automatically imported into LeanIX.
• These processes are available in LeanIX for reference and linkage to other architectural elements (e.g., applications, capabilities, initiatives).
• If an application has been assigned to a process step in BIC, this assignment is also synchronized to LeanIX Application Export from LeanIX to BIC • All active IT products in LeanIX are synchronized to BIC as applications.
• These applications can then be assigned to process activities within BIC.
This bidirectional integration ensures consistency between process modeling and enterprise architecture views.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Zusätzliche Relationen
CONTENT:
In addition to the automatically existing assignments between IT product and process, which are synchronized from BIC, business capabilities and modeling initiatives can be assigned to a process. Manual assignment of IT products via LeanIX should be avoided and should be done exclusively in BIC!
Further Information Detailed technical and functional information about the synchronization between LeanIX and BIC can be found in the chapter LeanIX System Integration with BIC.
