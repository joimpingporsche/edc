TITLE: Definition: Tech Capability
CONTENT:
A Technical Capability represents a functional classification of IT components based on what the technology enables. It describes the core function or purpose of a technology, such as a database, operating system, or server software. Unlike rapidly evolving technologies, the technical capability model remains relatively stable over time and is industry-agnostic.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Purpose and Benefits
CONTENT:
The primary purpose of modeling Technical Capabilities is to analyze the technical structure of the IT landscape and derive actionable insights. By assigning IT components to specific capabilities, organizations can:
• Increase transparency across the technology stack • Identify redundancies in the IT landscape • Detect whitespots (missing or underrepresented capabilities) Use Cases and Stakeholder Value • Product Teams can use the capability model to identify suitable IT components for their solutions.
• Domain Architects and Enterprise Architects can leverage it to optimize the IT portfolio, reduce duplication, and identify gaps in the technology landscape.
• Business Stakeholders benefit from a clearer understanding of how technology supports business needs, enabling better alignment between IT and business strategy.
