TITLE: Definition: IT Component
CONTENT:
An IT Component is a foundational element within the technical architecture of an IT product. It operates independently of any business logic or context. IT components can either be:
• Commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) products acquired from external vendors, or • Custom-developed software created in-house.
They serve as the technical building blocks that enable the functionality and operation of IT products.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Purpose and benefits
CONTENT:
IT components are essential to run, maintain, and change IT products. They may be:
• Encapsulated within technical platforms, or • Directly integrated into the IT product.
The use of IT components supports strategic architecture management by enabling:
• Lifecycle planning of technical assets • Clear documentation of ownership, responsibilities, and competencies • Transparency in the technical landscape • Identification of redundancies (overengineering) or gaps (underengineering) in technical capabilities • Discovery of synergies that can inform IT planning and optimization Accurate and complete documentation of IT components also contributes to operational stability and reliability.
