TITLE: Overview
CONTENT:
Architectural and regulatory information systems are modeled in LeanIX across all TOGAF architecture levels. This is achieved through the creation, maintenance, and assignment of architectural objects.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Architecture Levels
CONTENT:
Architecture Vision At this level, strategy and transformation are represented through:
• Objectives • Initiatives (Projects) Business Architecture This layer includes the following objects:
• Organization • Business Capability • Process Note: Except for Business Capabilities and their mappings, these elements are created and maintained outside of LeanIX as part of business process modeling.
Information Systems Architecture This central layer maps the implementation of business capabilities. It includes:
• Digital Solution • IT Product (Application, Platform, Group Application) • Interface These are part of the Application Architecture. Additionally:
• Data Objects are managed in the Information Architecture layer.
[SECTION_END]

TITLE: Architectural Objects and Attributes
CONTENT:
Each object in LeanIX is represented by a dedicated Fact Sheet. These are interconnected through mappings to form a comprehensive, domain-oriented, and technical architecture.
The attributes of each object are based on the LeanIX standard data model and are extended with Porsche-specific attributes, especially in the areas of:
• Security • Data Protection • Governance Each Fact Sheet type includes mandatory fields required to meet PASS minimum standards.
These fields are clearly marked.
Page Structure and Navigation To ensure consistency and readability, all pages follow the same structure and provide context- specific content.
Main Page Each architectural object is described in the following sections:
• Definition: Explains what the object represents, based on standard definitions and Porsche-specific characteristics.
• Purpose and Benefit: Describes the role and value of the object in architecture modeling.
Attributes Subpage Details the attributes of the object, aligned with the corresponding Fact Sheet.
Modeling Subpage Explains how the object should be modeled, where modeling takes place, and which roles are responsible. It includes Porsche-specific modeling guidelines and links to LeanIX standard modeling practices.
Recommendation: Follow LeanIX standard guidelines unless Porsche-specific deviations apply.
