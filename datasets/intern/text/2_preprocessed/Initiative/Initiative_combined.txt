TITLE: Definition: Initiative
CONTENT:
An Initiative represents a targeted, time-bound effort that drives change within the organization. It requires dedicated resources, impacts the enterprise architecture, and contributes directly to the achievement of one or more defined objectives.
Initiatives serve as the operational link between strategic intent and execution, enabling measurable progress toward business goals.

TITLE: Purpose and Benefits of the Initiative Fact
CONTENT:
Sheet Visibility and Impact Analysis Modeling an Initiative Fact Sheet provides a clear view of the status, scope, and impact of an initiative across the enterprise architecture. It enables stakeholders to assess how an initiative affects key architectural domains, including:
• Business Capabilities • Business Contexts • Applications • Organizations • Platforms • IT Components
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Strategic Alignment and Traceability
CONTENT:
Each initiative can be linked to one or more Objectives, allowing for:
• Transparent tracking of how initiatives contribute to strategic goals.
• Improved alignment between operational execution and business strategy.
[SECTION_END]

TITLE: Operational Insights
CONTENT:
The Initiative Fact Sheet also supports the surfacing of relevant metadata, such as:
• Budget information • Timeline and milestones • Responsible units or roles This structured view enables better decision-making, prioritization, and communication across business and IT.
