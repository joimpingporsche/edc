TITLE: Definition: Business Capability
CONTENT:
A business capability defines what an organization must be able to do to fulfill its mission and deliver value. It represents a stable, organization-wide function that is independent of specific processes, systems, or organizational structures.
Enterprise architects use business capabilities to:
• Capture cross-functional needs across business units.
• Provide a common language for strategic planning and transformation.
• Identify the technologies and resources required to support those capabilities.
Business capabilities form a foundational layer in enterprise architecture, enabling alignment between business strategy and IT execution.

[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Differentiation from Business Processes
CONTENT:
Business capabilities and business processes serve distinct purposes:
• Capabilities describe what the organization must be able to do.
• Processes describe how those capabilities are executed.
While overlaps may occur, the abstraction level and modeling intent are fundamentally different.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Analytical and Operational Value
CONTENT:
A well-maintained capability model enables:
• Identification of gaps ("white spots") in the IT landscape.
• Detection of redundant application support for the same capability.
• Capability-based application portfolio optimization.
[SECTION_END]
[SECTION_START]
TITLE: Lifecycle and Strategic Planning
CONTENT:
With sufficient data quality and maintenance, business capabilities support:
• Lifecycle tracking of application support per capability.
• Planning of transitions and investments in business units or portfolios.
• Strategic alignment reporting by linking capabilities to applications and corporate goals.
